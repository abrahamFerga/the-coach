# Industry research: fitness

## Top commercial players

1. **ABC Trainerize** — The market leader in personal training software; full-stack platform covering workout delivery, nutrition, habits, payments, and a branded client app. Founded 2012 (acquired by ABC Fitness). ~400,000 coaches, 1.6M client users. Segment: SMB to mid-market.
2. **My PT Hub** — All-in-one coaching platform with the broadest feature set at the SMB tier; strong nutrition database and scheduling tools. Founded 2015. ~130,000 coaches. Segment: SMB.
3. **Everfit** — Scale-first platform designed for group programming at volume; standout sport/athlete analytics and AI macro scanning. Founded 2019. ~200,000 coaches, 190+ countries. Segment: SMB to mid-market.
4. **PT Distinction** — Automation-heavy platform favoured by coaches running programmatic drip businesses; deepest SMS/email multi-channel outreach and workflow tooling. Founded 2013. Customer count undisclosed but Capterra "User Favourite" badge. Segment: SMB.
5. **TrueCoach** — Focused, clean one-on-one coaching tool; strong wearable ecosystem (WHOOP, OURA, Garmin) and athlete data integrations. Founded 2015. ~16,000–20,000 coaches. Segment: SMB (solo trainers and boutique studios).

(Five players cover the SMB-to-mid-market segment cleanly. Enterprise gym management — Mindbody, Glofox — operates on a structurally different axis and is excluded.)

## Synthesized capabilities

### Must-have (v1)
Capabilities present in at least 4 of 5 players — table-stakes for any entrant.

- **Workout programming & builder** — drag-and-drop or structured program builder; core of every platform.
- **Exercise library with video demonstrations** — coaches expect 3,000–8,000 exercises with embedded video; custom uploads required.
- **Training program templates** — reusable program blocks coaches apply across clients.
- **Nutrition tracking (macros / calories)** — calorie and macro targets, logging, and progress graphs.
- **Habit tracking** — daily check-boxes for sleep, water, steps, and coach-defined custom habits.
- **Progress photos** — time-stamped client photo uploads visible to coach.
- **Body metrics tracking** — weight, body fat %, measurements over time with trend charts.
- **Client dashboard / profiles** — centralized view of each client's programs, compliance, and metrics.
- **Compliance / adherence monitoring** — at-a-glance roster view showing who is on-track vs. slipping.
- **Automated check-ins & assessment forms** — scheduled questionnaires that surface client feedback.
- **In-app 1:1 messaging** — real-time chat between coach and client inside the platform.
- **Group programs & challenges** — deliver a single program to many clients with individual modifications.
- **Payment processing (recurring + one-time)** — Stripe-backed subscriptions and one-off package sales.
- **Wearable integrations** — pull activity, heart rate, sleep, and HRV from Apple Health, Garmin, WHOOP, OURA.
- **Team / multi-coach support** — assign clients to staff coaches; role-based permissions.

### Differentiator (v1)
Capabilities deep in 1–2 players — the "why us" features. Picking 3.

- **AI workout + meal plan generation** — deep in Trainerize and PT Distinction; emerging elsewhere. Coaches who generate programs at scale gain 40–60% time savings. Strong pull factor for solos running 30+ client rosters.
- **Workflow automations with drip messaging** — PT Distinction is the standout; Everfit solid. Automated onboarding sequences, scheduled content delivery, and re-engagement drips separate process-driven coaches from manual-follow-up ones. Reduces coach churn, not just client churn.
- **Sport / athlete performance analytics** — Everfit only (deep); TrueCoach basic. Training load charts, HRV trends, readiness scores, sport-specific drill tagging. Untapped for coaches working with competitive athletes vs. general fitness clients.

### Skip for v1
Capabilities that are either niche, low-ROI, or duplicated by off-shelf tools clients already use.

- **SMS + email outreach** — PT Distinction differentiator, but adds compliance (TCPA, CAN-SPAM) and deliverability complexity; coaches can use MailChimp/ActiveCampaign bridges. Integrate via webhook in v2.
- **Appointment & class booking** — only 2 players deep; trainers default to Calendly or Google Calendar. The market treats this as peripheral; it's a gym-management feature, not a coaching-software feature.
- **Video calls / live coaching** — only Trainerize and My PT Hub; clients use Zoom/FaceTime. Building a native video product is infrastructure-heavy and adds no competitive edge.
- **Client marketplace / public discovery** — acquisition channel, not product feature. Adds two-sided marketplace complexity to v1. Out of scope.
- **Custom white-label branded app** — 4/5 players offer it, but it's an enterprise upsell (App Store submission, MDM, etc.). Default to a coach-branded web portal in v1; native app is v2.

## Notable UX patterns observed

- **Workout-first mobile feed** — every platform opens the client app to "today's workout." The client inbox is workout-centric, not message-centric. Seen in: all five players.
- **Coach console / client app split** — coaches use a desktop-heavy web console for programming; clients use a mobile app for logging. The two surfaces share data but have entirely separate UX. Seen in: all five players.
- **Traffic-light compliance roster** — coaches see a roster sorted by last-logged date with color coding (green = active, amber = 2–3 days silent, red = 4+ days). Driving force behind daily coach engagement. Seen in: Trainerize, My PT Hub, Everfit, PT Distinction.
- **Program block structure** — programs are organized into named blocks (e.g., "Week 1 – Hypertrophy") with a linear calendar view; progressive overload is expressed as block-to-block weight/rep increases. Seen in: Trainerize, PT Distinction, TrueCoach.
- **Set-by-set logging UX** — clients log each set in real time during the workout, with auto-populated previous weights as defaults. The logging screen is the most-used surface in the client app. Seen in: all five players.
- **Weekly check-in modal** — a recurring lightweight questionnaire (sleep, stress, energy, motivation 1–10 plus an open field) that arrives Friday or Sunday. Data feeds the coach's dashboard as trend lines. Seen in: Trainerize, PT Distinction, Everfit, My PT Hub.

## Compliance / regulatory considerations

- **GDPR (EU Regulation 2016/679)** — health-related metrics (weight, body fat, photos, workout performance) are personal data; require lawful basis for processing, right-to-erasure, data portability. Constrains schema design: soft-delete everywhere; export API required.
- **Health data privacy (non-HIPAA)** — fitness platforms are not HIPAA-covered entities (no medical diagnosis), but several US states (California CMIA, Virginia VCDPA) extend health data protections to wellness apps. Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3) is the minimum.
- **PCI DSS** — card payment processing requires PCI compliance. Stripe handles card data; the platform must never log raw card numbers, must pass SAQ A (redirect model) or SAQ A-EP (embedded form).
- **COPPA / age gating** — fitness platforms must not collect data from users under 13 without parental consent (US). Age gate on sign-up required; no minor accounts in v1.
- **Wearable data brokering** — Apple HealthKit terms prohibit selling or transferring health data to third parties or using it for advertising. Any wearable integration must route through the user's explicit consent and must not share data outside the platform.

## Open questions for the user

1. **Target segment** — solo personal trainers (1–30 clients), coaching studios (1–10 coaches, 30–300 clients), or enterprise gyms? The UX, pricing model, and multi-tenancy depth differ substantially between these three.
   **Answer:** All segments — enterprise gyms, coaching studios, solo trainers, and standalone individual users. The platform is fully multi-tenant and configurable per tenant type, serving each segment with the right feature surface.

2. **Remote vs. hybrid** — remote-only coaching (no in-person session tracking), or should the platform support hybrid (in-person session check-in, live session logging with a coach present)?
   **Answer:** Both remote and hybrid. Mode is configurable per tenant and per session.

3. **Geographic focus** — global English-speaking, US-first, or LATAM? Mexico-first (like `the-ledger`) would add Spanish-locale requirements and peso pricing.
   **Answer:** US-first.

4. **Athlete tier in v1?** — Sport/athlete performance analytics (training load, HRV, sport-specific drill tagging) is a clear differentiator but narrows the TAM. Include in v1 or defer to v2?
   **Answer:** Include in v1.

5. **Monetization model** — per-coach subscription (most common), per-client seat pricing, or tiered by feature set? Hybrid (base per-coach + overage per client) is emerging.
   **Answer:** Per-tenant subscription, tiered by tenant type — individual user, solo trainer, coaching studio, and enterprise gym each have their own plan tier with appropriate feature gates and pricing.
